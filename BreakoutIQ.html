Risk Rules (Module 9)

  • 🔴2% Rule — Never risk more than 2% of total capital on any single trade (₹2,200 max risk)
  • 🟡5% Daily Stop — If daily P&L hits -5% (₹5,500), stop trading for the day
  • 🟢Max 3 Positions — Never hold more than 3 open positions at once with ₹1.10L capital
  • 🔵1:2 Minimum RR — Only enter trades where reward is at least 2x the risk. 1:3 is ideal.
  • 🟣Position Reserve — Keep 10% capital (₹11,000) as buffer for brokerage, taxes, and slippage
  • ⚡GTT Orders — Set stop-loss via GTT on Upstox immediately after entry. Never watch manually.

Pre-Trade Entry Checklist

Module 2 + Module 10 — Run this before every trade

  • Consolidation Zone — Stock has been sideways for 5–15 trading days in a tight range (less than 5% range)
  • Clean Resistance Level — The breakout level has been tested 2–3 times previously. More tests = stronger breakout when it breaks.
  • Volume Confirmation — Breakout candle volume is at least 1.5x the 20-day average volume
  • Nifty Health Check — Nifty50 is not in a downtrend (above its 20-EMA). Avoid buying breakouts in a falling index.
  • RSI Zone — RSI(14) is between 50–70. Below 50 = weak momentum. Above 80 = overbought risk.
  • Entry Price Valid — Buying within 1% of the breakout level. Not chasing a stock that's already moved 3–5%.
  • Risk:Reward ≥ 1:2 — Stop-loss and target set. Reward must be at least 2x the risk before entering.
  • Position Size Calculated — Used the 2% Rule to determine exact number of shares. Not a round guess.

Exit Checklist

Module 10 — Know when to get out

  • Target 1 Hit — Book 50% of position at +5 to +7%. Set trailing stop on rest.
  • 5-Day Rule — No movement in 5 days? Exit. Capital is better deployed elsewhere.
  • Friday Exit — Exit open positions by Friday close to avoid weekend gap risk.
  • Index Red Flag — If Nifty drops 1.5%+ in a session, exit all open long positions immediately.
  • Volume Dry Up — If stock breaks down on high volume — exit without waiting for stop to hit.

Trading Psychology (Module 9)

  • 🧠No revenge trading after a loss. Take a 30-min break minimum.
  • 🧠Never average down on a losing position. If stop is hit, accept and move on.
  • 🧠Paper trade first — Run this system for 30 days on paper before deploying real capital.
  • 🧠Focus on process — A losing trade with the right process is better than a winning trade with the wrong process.
  • 🧠Journal every trade — Emotions included. Pattern recognition improves your system over time.
